What is Mallakhamb?

 

Mallakhamb is an ancient Indian sport that blends strength, agility, flexibility, and focus. Performed on a vertical wooden pole or hanging rope, it involves acrobatic poses, yoga postures, and gymnastic transitions—all executed with grace and control.

 

Derived from the words “Malla” (wrestler) and “Khamb” (pole), this discipline was historically used to train wrestlers in body coordination and mental discipline.

A Sport Rooted in Tradition

 

Though its origins date back to the 12th century (and perhaps earlier), Mallakhamb continues to evolve. Today, it is recognized as a competitive sport with structured formats like Pole Mallakhamb, Rope Mallakhamb, and Hanging Mallakhamb.

 

Yet, at its heart, it remains a cultural treasure—an art form that carries the soul of Indian physical training.
